‚ö° TRANSMISSION BEGINS ‚ö°

We were somewhere around Week 3 of the bootcamp, deep in the HTML and CSS trenches, when the flexbox began to take hold. I remember saying something like "I feel a bit lightheaded; maybe you should explain display: grid again..." And suddenly there was a terrible roar all around us and the sky was full of what looked like huge nested div elements, all swooping and screeching and diving around the VSCode editor, which was now open across three monitors and spinning at a terrible frequency.

The CSS was refusing to center anything. Margin: auto had stopped working. Padding and margin were locked in an existential battle for dominance, and I was just a witness to the carnage.

Then the DevTools started barking: "OVERFLOW: HIDDEN! Z-INDEX: 9999! POSITION: ABSOLUTE!"

And a voice was screaming: "Holy Jesus! Why is there a 1px gap and where the hell is it coming from?"

I reached for my coffee. Then my wine. Sometimes in that order. Sometimes not.

This journey runs on two things and two things only: Coffee in the morning to convince myself I understand CSS specificity, and wine in the evening to forget that I ever tried to center a div. üíÄ CODING HORRORS: A DOCUMENTATION

  • The Stare: My code works... but only when I stare at it menacingly. I've tried looking away. The layout breaks. I look back. It works. I've accepted that I'm now in a committed relationship with my viewport. It's toxic but we make it work.
  • The Hydra Effect: Tried to fix one bug. Created 10 more. Called it "progress." Named them all. They're my children now. Bug #7 is my favorite‚Äîit only appears on Tuesdays and when Mercury is in retrograde.
  • The Flex Paradox: Spent 4 hours trying to understand why my flex container wouldn't flex. Turns out I spelled it "fles." The computer is VERY literal. Who knew?
  • The Z-Index Wars: Currently engaged in a cold war with z-index values. I'm up to z-index: 99999. My navbar is now technically in orbit. Can see it from the International Space Station. Still not sure if it's in front of the modal.
  • The Responsive Nightmare: Built a beautiful desktop layout. Looked at it on mobile. Cried. Everything is now position: absolute. I don't know why. I'm afraid to change it. We don't talk about mobile anymore.

"Yes, I know it works. NO, THAT DOESN'T MAKE IT RIGHT! And stop with the position: absolute on EVERYTHING! That's not responsive design, that's CHAOS design!"
